Project 1: Pancake Printer

CNC Frame

The first step in making an automatic pancake printer was a custom corexy CNC frame

Extruder

Much like a 3d printer, a pancake printer needs an extruder. Just this one is a custom perestaltic pump made from a stepper motor, for fine, volumetric control.

Project 2: Home Automation

Lights

I wanted a way to control the main light in my room from a button or a voice command without interfering with the ability to use the light switch as normal. The primative but efficient solution I found was to use a high torque servo controlled by a RF enabled arduino.

Heater

Living in Wisconsin during the winter means cold, cold mornings. I wanted a way to have my wall mounted heater turn on a few hours before I woke each day so that I could wake up to a toasty room. Again using a high torque servo, I mated a printed gear to the servo and another to the knob on the heater, and controlled the position of the servo with an ESP8366

Windows

Expanding on the routine of heating my room each morning, I decided I would like sunlight as well, necessitating automated window shades. This solution came in the form of a spool connected to a high-torque dc motor, that could spool the window shade chords in or out as needed.

Decor

Finally, I linked all the previous automations to a central controller, in this case a photon. To capitalize more on the photon's capabilities, I made a laser-cut box with a painting by Bob Ross on the front, through with a set of LEDs could shine, creating a backlit painting. While I had the code, I also added LED strips throughout my room, all controlled by the photon.
